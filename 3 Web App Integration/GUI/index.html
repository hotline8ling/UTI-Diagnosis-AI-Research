Urinalyze: UTI Prediction Model

UTIs (Urinary Tract Infections) are one of the most common bacterial infections, often leading to serious complications if not diagnosed accurately and promptly. Our innovative approach uses machine learning to enhance UTI diagnosis, applying advanced models like TabNet, XGBoost, and SVM with optimized tuning for precision. Through SHAP analysis and a soft voting ensemble of top models, this framework boosts diagnostic accuracy and reliability, supporting better, faster UTI management. Explore how our technology is setting new standards in healthcare diagnostics.

About

Our UTI diagnostic framework leverages a range of advanced machine learning models to enhance prediction accuracy and reliability.

Support Vector Machine (SVM)

A model that works well with high-dimensional data, finding the optimal boundary between UTI-positive and UTI-negative cases. SVM enhances diagnostic reliability, even with smaller or complex datasets.

XGBoost

A powerful gradient boosting algorithm known for its speed and accuracy, especially in structured data tasks. XGBoost helps detect UTI patterns efficiently with high precision, reducing the risk of misdiagnosis.

TabNet

A deep learning model that captures complex patterns in data, ideal for tabular data like medical records. TabNetâ€™s interpretability makes it easier to understand which features influence UTI predictions.

Logistic Regression

A classic, interpretable model that predicts the likelihood of a UTI. Logistic Regression serves as a solid baseline model, providing a straightforward approach to understanding diagnostic probabilities.

LightGBM

A gradient boosting model optimized for speed and efficiency. LightGBM is well-suited for large datasets, making it effective in handling extensive patient data for UTI prediction.

Ensemble Model

Our ensemble model enhances UTI diagnosis by integrating machine learning techniques like TabNet, XGBoost, SVM, LightGBM, and Logistic Regression. Utilizing a soft voting mechanism, this approach combines the strengths of top-performing models to improve prediction accuracy. Each model undergoes hyperparameter tuning with Optuna, and SHAP analysis identifies key features that drive predictions. This efficient methodology delivers reliable UTI detection, supporting better clinical decision-making and patient outcomes.

Features

Real-time Diagnostics

Capable of providing quick and accurate predictions to support timely clinical decisions, improving patient management and care.

Advanced Predictive Accuracy

Utilizes state-of-the-art algorithms (TabNet, XGBoost, SVM, LightGBM, Logistic Regression) to provide highly accurate UTI predictions, reducing the risk of misdiagnosis.

Ensemble Learning

Combines the strengths of multiple models through a soft voting mechanism, enhancing overall prediction reliability and performance.

Hyperparameter Optimization

Implements Optuna for systematic hyperparameter tuning, ensuring each model operates at its best for improved outcomes.

Feature Importance Analysis

Employs SHAP analysis to identify and prioritize the most influential features in the dataset, enabling focused predictions and increased interpretability.

Robust Performance Metrics

Assesses model effectiveness using comprehensive metrics, including AUC, precision, recall, accuracy, and F1 score, to ensure reliable diagnostic performance.
